Privacy Policy for Next.js Performance Analyzer

Last Updated: June 1, 2023

Introduction

The Next.js Performance Analyzer ("we", "our", or "the extension") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and the choices you have regarding your information.

Information We Do Not Collect

Our extension does not collect, store, or transmit any personal information or browsing data from your device or browser, including:

  • Personal identifiers such as your name, email address, or IP address
  • Authentication information or passwords
  • Search history or browsing habits
  • Form data or input fields
  • Cookies or other tracking technologies
  • Web browsing history

Information We Process Locally

The Next.js Performance Analyzer operates entirely within your browser and processes the following information locally:

  • The structure and content of the current webpage you're visiting (to analyze if it's built with Next.js)
  • Performance metrics such as Largest Contentful Paint (LCP), Interaction to Next Paint (INP), and Cumulative Layout Shift (CLS)
  • Resource loading statistics to calculate speed scores
  • Next.js configuration data detected on the current page

All analysis is performed locally within your browser. No information is sent to external servers or third parties.

How We Use Information

The information processed by our extension is used solely to:

  • Detect if a website is built with Next.js
  • Analyze performance metrics of the current page
  • Generate recommendations for performance improvements
  • Display results to you within the extension popup

Data Storage

Any results from the analysis are stored temporarily in browser memory while the extension is active. This data is cleared when you navigate away from the page or close your browser.

Third-Party Access

We do not share any information with third parties. All processing is done locally within your browser.

Extension Permissions

Our extension requires the following permissions:

  • activeTab: To access and analyze the current tab when you click on the extension icon
  • scripting: To execute scripts required for performance analysis
  • Host permissions (http://*/* and https://*/*): To allow the extension to run on all websites you visit

These permissions are used solely for the purpose of analyzing Next.js websites and not for collecting or transmitting data.

Children's Privacy

Our extension does not collect any personal information from users of any age, including children under 13.

Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update our Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by posting the new Privacy Policy on the Chrome Web Store and updating the "Last Updated" date at the top of this page.
